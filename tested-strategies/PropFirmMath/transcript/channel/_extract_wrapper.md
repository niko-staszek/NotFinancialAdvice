## 5. THE "5 RULES" (`RGYbTD1j628.txt`) — verbatim list

1. **Only trade one strategy.** Need one trusted, proven-edge model. Strategy-hopping mid-drawdown "cuts off your edge mid strategy" on both → you capture the worst parts of two strategies. Only way to realize a strategy's EV is to see it through good and bad stretches.
2. **Attack the evaluations aggressively.** An eval is not a funded account — **risk is capped, no real downside, upside >> capped risk.** Most traders come in "too timid, undersizing, afraid of failing — that fear makes you more likely to fail." Job = pass as fast and efficiently as possible.
3. **Run multiple accounts across multiple firms simultaneously.** One funded account → every trade feels enormous → bad decisions. 10-15+ accounts → profit compounds, pressure per trade drops. "You have to be very organized... one of the most powerful advantages a prop firm can offer."
4. **Every account gets a daily profit target AND a hard daily loss limit.** Know both numbers before opening a chart. Hit target → walk away; hit stop → walk away, come back tomorrow. Prevents overtrading good days / revenge-trading bad days — both bleed EV.
5. **Psychology — "$3,000 in a trade needs to feel like $3."** Reframe as risking **one unit**, not a dollar amount — a predefined calculated risk based on account + edge. Once placed, the dollar figure is irrelevant. With rules 1-4 in place, psychology is the only thing left that can sabotage you.
