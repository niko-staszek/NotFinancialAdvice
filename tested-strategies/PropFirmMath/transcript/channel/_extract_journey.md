## FAIR-PRICE RESET (when the fair-value reference updates mid-session)

This is the biggest payload across the corpus. Consistent rule: **scheduled/priced-in news → fade back to pre-news price; unexpected news (tweets) or regime-changing news → the post-news consolidation becomes the NEW fair price (do NOT fade it).**

- **Unexpected = new fair price; consolidation marks it.** "when you have Trump tweets it comes in like this random Trump tweet the consolidation after because it's unexpected. This would be a new fair price. So I would not try to revert that." (CzxSgYujDxs)
- **Scheduled red-folder news = priced in = fade it.** "normally when you have scheduled news like red folder news at like 8:30 a.m. Eastern... It was already priced in... that's why I love to revert the red folder news." (CzxSgYujDxs)
- **FOMC explicitly fade-able.** "FOMC is always priced in so well... when it comes out and it's exactly as forecasted, then this entire move down is unfair." Pre-FOMC price = fair, fade back to it. (xTxrtFKlQfc)
- **8:15 vs 8:30 stacking:** "the fair price is going to be the pre-8:15 news because a move away from 8:15 is unfair... the 8:30 there's no fair price here because it was already determined by the 8:15 candle." (Lep139B5EbU)
- **Regime-changing news (war resolution) resets fair price upward — news drift, do NOT mean-revert:** "the fair price of Nasdaq futures have most likely changed because the outcome from the war... changes the fair price... the most recent consolidation is usually what I would term to be the fair price." (RnwOqmEF6Rk)
- **Reset rule via volume direction (intraday, no news):** when a fresh volume spike pushes price, follow where the volume went to relocate fair price. "I'm going to change the fair price down to Asia open because when the volume came in, it went down. It did not go up towards there." Repeats: "when the volume spike of these candles came in, it went down... I would then treat this most recent consolidation as a fair price." (CzxSgYujDxs)
- **Two-candidate fair price → mark the MIDDLE:** "I honestly just put a line in the middle of all of them and then I treated that as the fair price... I knew for sure it's either here or here. So why not mark the middle and I'll be right most of the time." (CzxSgYujDxs)
- **Earnings are special** (forecast partly priced, beat/miss not): default = play news drift (buy green earnings immediately); if it starts dumping, earnings were priced in → short the whole move back down. (CzxSgYujDxs)
- **10am / 2pm scheduled news also reset fair price** to the pre-news price intraday (multiple instances, CzxSgYujDxs, gjWiGBiVis0, Lep139B5EbU).

## CONTINUATION-CYCLE detail (continuations beyond the first candle)

- **Continuation window = first 15 min only.** "one final one within the first 15 minutes is when I'm done with continuations." / "16 minutes after the open I usually stop continuations after 15 minutes." (gjWiGBiVis0, xTxrtFKlQfc)
- **Continuation requires break+close past structure IN the opening-candle direction.** Only take continuations matching opening-candle color/bias; skip if opening candle is tiny/unbiased (body ~4 pts → "market doesn't really have a strong bias... not in favor of taking continuations on those days"). (xTxrtFKlQfc)
- **Continuation gets the 1:1.5 (38/25) leg; reversion gets the same or larger.** Continuation TP = 38 (50k) / 75 (150k). (multiple)
- **Repeating-continuation / "keep sending" on trend (the closest thing to a continuation CYCLE):** on high-volume one-way moves he keeps re-entering every new break of structure in the move's direction: "Whenever it breaks structure in my favor, I'm just going to send something in. I don't really care if it wins or loses... on average, it's expected to go down. You're going to make money as it goes down." Notes most of these individually LOSE but the few big winners pay. (CzxSgYujDxs)
- **News-drift = a slow multi-hour continuation** (overnight bullish drift after bullish news); explicitly a separate mode from the 15-min continuation: "more of a news drift trade just wanted to slow rise over the day," exited break-even at market close. (xTxrtFKlQfc, RnwOqmEF6Rk)

## Concrete entry / exit / session / sizing rules

- **Entries:** funded accounts → wait for **break of structure** (stronger). Evals → allow **displacement-candle** entries (more setups, more aggressive). "On evals I will take displacement candle entries, but funded accounts I like to wait for break of structure." (xTxrtFKlQfc, gjWiGBiVis0)
- **Structure defined:** "structure is when a wick is taller than the two next to it." Break = close beyond it. (gjWiGBiVis0)
- **Displacement defined:** the move candle(s) must be bigger than / close beyond the opposing candle's body+wick (he counts 1–5 candles being displaced/closed-through). (CzxSgYujDxs)
- **Base risk:** 25-pt stop / 38-pt TP (=1:1.5) on **50k** accounts. **150k** accounts → double it: **50-pt stop / 75–76-pt TP**. (Lep139B5EbU, RnwOqmEF6Rk, gjWiGBiVis0)
- **Opening-candle-size sizing gate:** if opening candle body **> 25 pts**, cut contract size in half and use a **50-pt stop / 76-pt TP** instead of 25/38. Stated repeatedly as a hard rule. "50 point stop only when the candle is more than 25 points on the open." (CzxSgYujDxs, xTxrtFKlQfc, gjWiGBiVis0)
- **High-volume discretion:** if >100 pts in your favor, optionally widen to 50-pt stop to cut variance — "it's the same in the long run... but it will definitely reduce variance." Size DOWN when lots of points in favor. (CzxSgYujDxs)
- **TP styles:** static round numbers (38/50/75/76/100) OR exact distance to fair price. "static risk is a little bit better than exact measurements on prop firms." Caps personal trades at ~100 pts max ("I tend not go for more than 100 as my longest trades"). (gjWiGBiVis0)
- **Stop time / trade cap:** **stop trading at 11:00 a.m.** AND/OR after **3 trades** (occasionally a 4th only if A+). Hard 11:00 cutoff cost him a 9k winner two minutes later — kept the rule anyway. (CzxSgYujDxs, Lep139B5EbU)
- **Break-even move:** go BE when (a) market open is about to print (avoid open-wick stop-out) or (b) news is about to release while in a trade. Otherwise doesn't use BE. (RnwOqmEF6Rk, Lep139B5EbU)
- **Sessions traded:** 6pm Asia open, 9:30 NY AM, 2pm NY PM. Each session's OPEN candle = that session's fair-price reference. Does NOT trade London. (CzxSgYujDxs)
- **Per-firm risk routing:** picks WHICH prop firm/account to trade on based on expected move, because each firm has a different optimal risk given its rules. "there must exist an optimal risk... different across each firm because they have different rules, so I'll trade a different account based on the sort of move I'm expecting." (Lep139B5EbU)
- **A+ continuation example:** Asia opens large green + HTF bias long (fill gap) + revert to 6pm fair price + break/close structure → "bet everything on that." (CzxSgYujDxs)
